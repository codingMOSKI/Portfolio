I chose to compare a clasic legendary novel moby dick, which came out in 1851, to a few old news articles from the 2000s. I was hoping to observe the differences in words and tone between a popular fiction novel to the news

I decided to observe the 2 and 3 word N-Grams to see what the trend was in word choice and topics. When it comes to moby dick , unsuprisingly, they all talk about a subject and most of them is about the whale.

Moby Dick has a trend of a majority of the time speaking about subjects and such and less of an action oriented way of writing.

Now for the news articles!

The word collage for the news article shows some trends in the word choice and topic, things like the government, uzbekistan, islamic, regime and rights display a clear focus on politics and such.

Taking a complete 180 from political and serious topics, the 2001 news article spends all of it's time talking about film and some man named Gaelan

compared to Moby Dick which has a very stable and constant line of topics and style the news articles are all over the place in both ways.

I decided to check out the 2000s article next and wow was I suprised, like I said the topics are all over the place, this article is back to important topics/ the government and treasure island for some reason.

In comparison to Moby dick the news article have a significant difference in variety and often more direct or action based words in opposition to Moby Dick's common us of "of the"
